Getting Started

Welcome! This guide will help you navigate and use all features of the system effectively.

What does this system do?

Our system uses advanced AI and machine learning to predict landslide risks. It analyzes multiple factors including:

  • Rainfall data: Cumulative and intensity measurements
  • Soil moisture: Ground saturation levels
  • Terrain analysis: Slope angles derived from DEM data
  • Soil composition: Different soil types and their properties

Using the Map

1. Selecting a Location

You can select a location in two ways:

  • Search: Type a city or province in the search box.
  • Click: Click anywhere on the map to select specific coordinates.
Tip: The map covers the entire Philippines. Zoom in and pan to explore different areas.
2. Understanding Map Information

When a location is selected, key geospatial data is displayed:

  • Location: The name of the selected area or its coordinates.
  • Slope: The slope angle of the terrain in degrees.
  • Soil Type: The soil composition at that location.

Making Predictions

Step-by-Step Process
1
Select Location

Search or click on the map.

2
Choose Date

Select a forecast date (up to 5 days ahead).

3
Select Period

Choose from 3 hours to 5 days.

4
Get Prediction

Click the "Predict Landslide" button.

Available Time Periods
  • 3 to 12 Hours: For short-term, immediate risk assessment.
  • 1 to 5 Days: For medium to long-term planning and preparedness.

Understanding Results

Prediction Result Explained
Landslide Predicted: A high probability of a landslide event. Take immediate precautions.
No Landslide Predicted: Conditions are currently stable with low probability.
Contributing Factors
Factor Description Impact
Rainfall Cumulative rainfall (mm) and intensity. High values significantly increase risk.
Soil Moisture Water content in the soil (%). Saturated soil is less stable.
Slope Angle of the terrain in degrees. Steeper slopes have higher risk.
Soil Type Composition and characteristics of the soil. Clay-heavy soils are more susceptible.

Dashboard Features

The dashboard provides comprehensive historical analysis and insights.

Key Metrics
  • Historical Occurrences: Yearly/monthly landslide counts.
  • Geospatial Map: Location of past landslide events.
  • Regional Hotspots: Regions with the highest frequency of landslides.
Factor Analysis
  • Soil Vulnerability: Count of landslides per soil type.
  • Slope Analysis: Relation of slope angle to landslide occurrences.
